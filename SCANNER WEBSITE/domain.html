Understanding Website Domains

Your ultimate guide to domain essentials

What is a Domain?

A domain is the unique address of a website on the internet, such as example.com. It allows users to access a website easily without remembering complex IP addresses.

Components of a Domain

  • Top-Level Domain (TLD): The extension of the domain, like .com, .org, .net, etc.
  • Second-Level Domain (SLD): The unique identifier of the domain, such as "example" in example.com.
  • Subdomain: A prefix to the domain, such as blog.example.com.

Domain Lifecycle

Every domain undergoes several stages in its lifecycle:

  • Available: The domain is free for registration.
  • Registered: The domain has been purchased by a user.
  • Active: The domain is being used.
  • Expired: The registration period has ended, but it can still be renewed within a grace period.
  • Deleted: The domain is removed from the registry and becomes available again.

How to Choose a Domain Name

When selecting a domain name, consider the following:

  • Make it short and memorable.
  • Ensure it reflects your brand or purpose.
  • Use relevant keywords for SEO optimization.
  • Choose an appropriate TLD (e.g., .com for commercial).

Tools to Analyze Domain Information

  • WHOIS Lookup: Provides domain registration details, such as registrar and owner information.
  • DNS Checker: Verifies domain DNS settings and records.
  • SSL Checker: Validates the SSL certificate of the domain.

Frequently asked questions

Domain registration costs vary depending on the TLD and registrar but typically range from $10 to $50 per year.

Once registered, the domain name itself cannot be changed, but you can register a new one and redirect traffic from the old domain.

A domain name is the core address of a website, such as "example.com". A URL (Uniform Resource Locator) is a full web address that includes the domain name as well as additional information, like the protocol (http:// or https://), paths, and parameters (e.g., "https://example.com/about-us").

To register a domain name, you need to choose a domain registrar (such as GoDaddy, Namecheap, or Google Domains) and check if the desired domain name is available. If it is, you can proceed with the registration by providing contact information and paying a fee, typically on an annual basis. Once registered, the domain name can be used for creating websites or email addresses.

A subdomain is a prefix added to a domain to create a separate section of a website. For example, "blog.example.com" where "blog" is the subdomain of "example.com". It helps organize content or create distinct sections within a website.
